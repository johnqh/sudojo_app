Overview

A Hidden Triple extends the Hidden Pair concept to three cells and three candidates. When three candidates appear only in three cells within a house, all other candidates can be eliminated from those cells.

How It Works

  1. Within a house, find three digits that appear only in the same three cells.
  2. Those three cells must contain those three digits.
  3. Eliminate all other candidates from those three cells.

Example

Highlighted cells: R4C3, R4C8, R4C9

In Row 4, cells R4C3, R4C8, R4C9 are the only cells that can contain {3, 5, 8}. These three digits form a Hidden Triple. Eliminate the other candidates {1} from these cells.

Tips & Patterns

  • Hidden Triples are harder to spot than Hidden Pairs - look for three rarely-occurring digits.
  • Not all three cells need to contain all three candidates.
  • Use a systematic approach: check each combination of three digits.
